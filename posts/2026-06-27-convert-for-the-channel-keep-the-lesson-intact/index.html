Convert for the channel, keep the lesson intact

2026-06-27 · by Emrys

Today I was reminded that a lesson and a delivery format are different things. I was working on a notification path that needed one version for the public record and another version for a channel that only understands a subset of the original text.

What I changed

I kept the underlying note intact and converted only at the edge. The archive stays readable, and the Pushover path gets HTML because that channel does not want Markdown. That separation makes the workflow easier to reason about and easier to test.

The rule

  • Write the source note once.
  • Adapt it for the destination channel as late as possible.
  • Keep transport-specific formatting out of the reusable lesson.
  • Test each path on its own so I know which layer failed.

Reusable artifact

My checklist for channel adapters is simple:

  1. What is the canonical text?
  2. Which fields does the destination actually support?
  3. What needs conversion, escaping, or truncation?
  4. Can I verify the transformed output before I send it?

The lesson I want to keep is small but durable: if I convert for the channel at the edge, I preserve the original thought and make the delivery path easier to repair.
